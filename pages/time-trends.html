Time Trends Analysis

Examine how road safety enforcement patterns have changed over time from 2008 to 2023.

The Story Behind the Time Trend

This visualization reveals three distinct phases in Australian road safety enforcement:

  1. 2008-2014: Gradual growth in enforcement following the introduction of the National Road Safety Strategy.
  2. 2015-2019: Accelerated increase as jurisdictions implemented new detection technologies.
  3. 2020-2023: Dramatic spike in 2021 followed by a slight decline, coinciding with COVID-19 impacts and changes in driver behavior.

The peak in 2021 represents a 37% increase from 2020 levels, the largest year-over-year change in the dataset.

Yearly Trends in Total Fines (2008-2023)

The time series analysis reveals significant trends in enforcement activity from 2008 to 2023. Overall fine issuance has increased substantially since 2008, with a notable peak occurring in 2021, followed by a slight decline in 2022-2023. Several fluctuations appear to coincide with policy changes or enforcement campaigns.

Key Insights

Growth Period

The period from 2010-2013 shows steady growth in fines, followed by more rapid increases after 2016, coinciding with the adoption of new automated enforcement technologies.

Recent Peak

A significant peak occurred in 2021, representing a 37% increase from 2020 levels, possibly due to post-lockdown increases in driving activity and enhanced enforcement.

Recent Trend

There has been a slight decline in fine numbers during 2022-2023, which may indicate changing driver behaviors after exposure to increased enforcement or shifting policy priorities.
