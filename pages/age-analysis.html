Distribution of Fines Across Age Groups

This chart shows the distribution of road safety enforcement fines across different age groups. The 40-64 age group accounts for the highest number of fines (over 600,000), followed by the 26-39 age group (approximately 430,000). The 17-25 age group, often assumed to be the highest risk, actually ranks third. The 0-16 age group has negligible fines, which is expected given limited driving in this demographic.

Key Insights

Middle-aged Adults

The 40-64 age group receives the highest number of fines (approximately 604,750), challenging conventional assumptions that younger drivers might be more heavily represented in violation statistics.

Young Adults

The 26-39 age group follows with approximately 430,000 fines, with the 17-25 group further behind at 298,300 fines, suggesting that experience alone doesn't necessarily lead to better compliance.

Senior Drivers

The 65+ age group shows significantly fewer fines than middle-aged adults, which may reflect reduced driving activity rather than necessarily better compliance behaviors.
