Distribution of Fines Across Age Groups

This chart shows the distribution of road safety enforcement fines across different age groups. The 40-64 age group accounts for the highest number of fines, followed by the 26-39 age group. The 0-16 age group has negligible fines, which is expected given limited driving in this demographic.

Key Insights

Middle-aged Adults

The 40-64 age group receives the highest number of fines (approximately 604,750), despite conventional assumptions that younger drivers might be more heavily represented.

Young Adults

The 26-39 age group follows with approximately 430,000 fines, suggesting a significant impact on this demographic as well.

Senior Drivers

The 65+ age group shows significantly fewer fines than middle-aged adults, despite potentially higher driving experience.
