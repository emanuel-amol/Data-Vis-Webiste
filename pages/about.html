üéØ Our Mission: Transform Data Into Action

Core Mission: Transform raw enforcement data into compelling narratives that reveal hidden patterns and insights to improve road safety policy and public awareness across Australia.

Road safety enforcement is a critical component of public health and safety policy in Australia. The Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE) has been collecting transport statistics since 1970, with detailed road safety enforcement data available from 2008.

Our project targets multiple stakeholders:

  • Policy Makers and Law Enforcement - Evidence-based resource allocation and strategy development
  • Road Safety Researchers - Comprehensive trend analysis and pattern identification
  • General Public - Accessible insights into road safety enforcement in their jurisdiction
  • Media and Advocates - Data-driven narratives for public discourse

üé® Design Philosophy

Our visualization approach prioritizes:

  • Narrative Structure: Each visualization tells part of a larger story
  • Progressive Disclosure: Users can explore from high-level insights to detailed data
  • Challenge Assumptions: Deliberately surface counterintuitive findings
  • Actionable Insights: Every finding includes policy implications
  • Interactive Exploration: Users can test their own hypotheses

üß† Discovery #1: The Experience Paradox

üì± Discovery #2: Technology Revolution Impact

The 2021 spike of 37% in total fines directly correlates with mobile phone detection camera deployment, revealing the true scale of distracted driving.

132% NSW increase (2019-2021)
